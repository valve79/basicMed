EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The purpose of the Aircraft Owners and Pilots Association's (AOPA) medical education course is to make available on the internet free of charge to all individuals a medical education course that meets the requirements of section 2307(c) of the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) Extension, Safety and Security Act of 2016 (FESSA) and Title 14, Code of Federal Regulations (CFR), part 68, "Requirements for Operating Certain Small Aircraft Without a Medical Certificate." AOPA's medical education course serves to educate pilots on conducting medical self-assessments and upon successful completion, electronically provide to the individual a certificate of course completion and transmit to the FAA information required by Section 2307(c) of FESSA. This Privacy Impact Assessment is being published as required by AOPA's agreement with the FAA to provide its medical education course to help pilots meet the requirements of Section 2307 of FESSA.

I. OVERVIEW AND INTENDED USE OF INFORMATION COLLECTED

The Aircraft Owners and Pilots Association is a not-for-profit organization dedicated to general aviation, headquartered in Frederick, Maryland. With hundreds of thousands of members, AOPA is the largest, most influential general aviation association in the world. AOPA works to secure the future of general aviation through advocating on behalf of our members, educating pilots, non-pilots, and policy makers alike, supporting activities that ensure the long-term health of general aviation, fighting to keep general aviation accessible to all. AOPA worked for years on behalf of its members to bring about third class medical reform, which was enacted by section 2307(c) of the FESSA and implemented by FAA regulations in 14 CFR part 68, which the FAA now refers to as "BasicMed."

AOPA is offering a free online medical education course to allow to pilots comply with Section 2307 of FESSA and the FAA's BasicMed rules, which require pilots to have completed a medical education course during the 24 calendar months before acting as pilot in command under the BasicMed rules and to demonstrate proof of completion of the course.

To be an approved medical education course for pilots, AOPA's medical education course must meet the following requirements of Section 2307(c) of FESSA and BasicMed rules:

  • Be available on the Internet free of charge;
  • Be developed and periodically updated in coordination with representatives of relevant nonprofit and not-for-profit general aviation stakeholder groups;
  • Educate pilots on conducting medical self-assessments;
  • Advise pilots on identifying warning signs of potential serious medical conditions;
  • Identify risk mitigation strategies for medical conditions;
  • Increase awareness of the impacts of potentially impairing over-the-counter and prescription drug medications;
  • Encourage regular medical examinations and consultations with primary care physicians;
  • Inform pilots of the regulations pertaining to the prohibition on operations during medical deficiency and medically disqualifying conditions.
  • Provide the checklist developed by the Federal Aviation Administration in accordance with 2307(b).
  • Upon successful completion of the course, electronically provide to the individual and transmit to the Federal Aviation Administration—
    • A certification of completion of the medical education course, which shall be retained in the individual's logbook and made available upon request, and shall contain the individual's name, address, and airman certificate number;
    • A release authorizing single access to the National Driver Register through a designated State Department of Motor Vehicles to furnish to the FAA information pertaining to the individual's driving record;
    • A certification by the individual that the individual is under the care and treatment of a physician if the individual has been diagnosed with any medical condition that may impact the ability of the individual to fly, as required under § 61.23(c)(3) of this chapter;
    • A form that includes—
      • The name, address, telephone number, and airman certificate number of the individual;
      • The name, address, telephone number, and State medical license number of the physician performing the comprehensive medical examination;
      • The date of the comprehensive medical examination; and
      • A certification by the individual that the checklist described in § 68.7 was followed and signed by the physician during the medical examination required by this section; and
    • A statement, which shall be signed by the individual certifying that the individual understands the existing prohibition on operations during medical deficiency by stating: "I understand that I cannot act as pilot in command, or any other capacity as a required flight crew member, if I know or have reason to know of any medical condition that would make me unable to operate the aircraft in a safe manner."

Upon successful completion of the medical education course and the individual's provision of the required information described above, AOPA will electronically provide to the individual a certificate of course completion and transmit to the FAA the information above, as required by Section 2307(c) of FESSA.

Successful completion of this AOPA medical education course within the past 24 calendar months satisfies the medical education course requirement in FAR 61.23(c) for an individual seeking to act as Pilot in Command under BasicMed regulations. Pilots operating under "BasicMed" regulations are required to retain the certificate of completion with his or her logbook and make it available upon request.

II. INFORMATION COLLECTED

  1. WHAT INFORMATION IS BEING COLLECTED?
    FESSA and the FAA's regulations require that upon successful completion of the course, the individual must provide the following information about themselves:
    • Full name;
    • Address;
    • Telephone number; and
    • Airman certificate number.

    The individual must provide the following information concerning the medical examination required by Section 2307:

    • Date of the examination;
    • Name of Physician;
    • Address of Physician;
    • Telephone number of Physician; and
    • State medical license number of Physician.

    The individual must also provide the following certifications/authorizations as required by Section 2307:

    • I certify that I am under the care and treatment of a physician if I have been diagnosed with any medical condition that may impact my ability to fly.
    • I certify that I am under the care of a State-licensed medical specialist if I have a clinically diagnosed neurological condition.
    • I certify that I am under the care of a State-licensed medical specialist if I have a clinically diagnosed mental health condition.
    • I certify that to the best of my knowledge, the Comprehensive Medical Examination Checklist was followed and signed by the physician who performed the comprehensive medical examination.
    • I understand that I cannot act as pilot in command, or any other capacity as a required flight crew member, if I know or have reason to know of any medical condition that would make me unable to operate the aircraft in a safe manner.
    • A release authorizing single access to the National Driver Register through a designated State Department of Motor Vehicles to furnish to the FAA information pertaining to the individual's driving record.
  2. WHAT ARE THE SOURCES OF THE INFORMATION COLLECTED?
    The individual user of the medical education course will directly provide the information collected and will be the only source of the information collected. AOPA will not provide any of the information collected.
  3. WHY IS THE INFORMATION BEING COLLECTED?
    Section 2307 of FESSA and 14 CFR § 68.3 Medical Education Course Requirements require that upon successful completion of the medical education course, AOPA electronically provide to the individual and transmit to the FAA:
    • A certification of completion of the medical education course, which shall be retained in the individual's logbook and made available upon request, and shall contain the individual's name, address, and airman certificate number;
    • A release authorizing single access to the National Driver Register through a designated State Department of Motor Vehicles to furnish to the FAA information pertaining to the individual's driving record;
    • A certification by the individual that the individual is under the care and treatment of a physician if the individual has been diagnosed with any medical condition that may impact the ability of the individual to fly, as required under 14 CFR § 61.23(c)(3) of this chapter;
    • A form that includes—
      • The name, address, telephone number, and airman certificate number of the individual;
      • The name, address, telephone number, and State medical license number of the physician performing the comprehensive medical examination;
      • The date of the comprehensive medical examination; and
      • A certification by the individual that the checklist described in 14 CFR § 68.7 was followed and signed by the physician during the medical examination required by this section; and
    • A statement, which shall be signed by the individual certifying that the individual understands the existing prohibition on operations during medical deficiency by stating: "I understand that I cannot act as pilot in command, or any other capacity as a required flight crew member, if I know or have reason to know of any medical condition that would make me unable to operate the aircraft in a safe manner."
  4. DO INDIVIDUALS HAVE THE OPPORTUNITY AND/OR RIGHT TO DECLINE TO PROVIDE DATA?
    Submission of the data is mandatory, except for your date of birth and email, which are optional.
  5. WHAT HAPPENS IF I DO NOT PROVIDE ALL THE INFORMATION REQUIRED TO BE COLLECTED BY AOPA?
    Failure to provide all required information will result in AOPA being unable to provide to you a certification of completion of the medical education course and transmit the information to the FAA as required by 2307(c). AOPA will only send information to the FAA for those records that are fully complete, meaning records for those persons who have successfully completed the AOPA training course, provided all mandatory information, and have completed all user attestations including the National Driver Register (NDR) Authorization/Release.
  6. ARE THERE ANY AGE REQUIREMENTS FOR INDIVIDUALS PROVIDING THE INFORMATION COLLECTED?
    All AOPA sites and services are intended for general aviator audiences. AOPA does not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 years of age. If we learn we have inadvertently collected such information, we will delete it.
  7. WHAT TECHNOLOGIES WILL BE USED TO COLLECT THE INFORMATION?
    The information is collected directly from the individual user, who will provide the information using an electronic form.

III. USE, ACCURACY, AND AMENDMENT OF THE INFORMATION COLLECTED

  1. HOW WILL AOPA USE THE INFORMATION COLLECTED?
    Except as provided below, AOPA it will not use or disclose the information provided by persons taking its medical education for purposes other than providing such information to the FAA in compliance with § 2307 of FESSA.

    If the individual provides a specific "opt-in" consent, AOPA may use the name, email address, and/or address provided by such individual to send a request to complete a survey and provide feedback concerning AOPA's medical education course.

    If the individual provides a specific "opt-in" consent, AOPA may use the name, email address, and/or address provided by such individual to send a reminder that § 2307(a)(5) of FESSA requires the individual to have completed a medical education course during the 24 calendar months before acting as pilot in command of a covered aircraft and demonstrate proof of completion of the course.

    If the individual provides a specific "opt-in" consent, AOPA may use the name, email address, and/or address provided by such individual to send any type of communication from AOPA. Information collected from individuals that opt in to receive any type of communication from AOPA will be subject to the AOPA Privacy Policy found at https://www.aopa.org/privacy-policy

    Providing or withdrawing opt-in consent as set forth above will not change elections for those individuals already subject to the AOPA Privacy Policy. Any email communications sent by AOPA will contain a prominently located "unsubscribe" hyperlink allowing the recipient to withdraw consent from receiving further communications from AOPA. Individuals may also request at any time to have their name, address, phone number, or e-mail address excluded from various types of communications received from AOPA, in accordance with the AOPA Privacy Policy found at https://www.aopa.org/privacy-policy

  2. CAN AOPA VERIFY THE ACCURACY OF ANY INFORMATION PROVIDED BY THE INDIVIDUAL?
    AOPA cannot verify the accuracy of any information provided by the individual. The individual providing the information is the sole source of the information and must ensure that the information is accurat.
  3. HOW LONG WILL THE INFORMATION COLLECTED BE RETAINED BY AOPA?
    Unless otherwise expressly authorized by the user through a voluntary "opt-in" feature, AOPA will only retain the information collected upon successful completion of the medical education course until the information is transmitted to the FAA, as required by Section 2307 of FESSA and 14 CFR § 68.3. AOPA will transmit the information collected to the FAA on the current or next federal workday, Monday through Friday.

    If a user has expressly authorized AOPA, through a voluntary "opt-in," information will be retained in accordance with the retention schedule described below at V. Administrative and Technological Controls, 1. What is the Retention Schedule Established For The Information Collected?

  4. HOW DO I ACCESS AND REVIEW ANY INFORMATION COLLECTED THAT IS RETAINED BY AOPA?
    Upon request by any user to gain access to their record or to any information pertaining to them which is retained by AOPA, AOPA will permit the user to review the record and have a copy made of all or any portion thereof in a form comprehensible to them. Such inquiries must be made in writing and sent to:

    Aircraft Owners and Pilots Association
    Attn: Privacy and Security Officer
    421 Aviation Way
    Frederick, Maryland 21701

    Included in the request must be the following:

    • Your name
    • Your mailing address;
    • Your phone number;
    • Your email address; and
    • A description of the information or record sought.

  5. HOW MAY I REQUEST AN AMENDMENT OF THE INFORMATION COLLECTED PERTAINING TO ME?
    Users may request amendment of any information pertaining to them which is retained by AOPA. However, amendment of any information that has been electronically transmitted to the FAA may require the user to repeat completion of the medical education course and provide the required information. To request an amendment or correction of information, you must submit your request in writing to:

    Aircraft Owners and Pilots Association
    Attn: Privacy and Security Officer
    421 Aviation Way
    Frederick, Maryland 21701

    Requests may also be sent via email to pilotassist@aopa.org

    Included in the request must be the following:

    • Your name;
    • Your mailing address;
    • Your telephone number;
    • Your email address;
    • Information to be amended or corrected (Be as specific as possible and explain why you believe that the information in question is not accurate, relevant, timely, or complete. Please note that a requester bears the burden of proving by the preponderance of the evidence that information is not accurate, relevant, timely, or complete.)

    After a valid request to amend or correct a record is received by AOPA's Privacy and Security officer, the following steps will be taken:

    • AOPA's Privacy and Security Officer acknowledges the amendment request by email or letter within ten (10) business days after receipt of such request.
    • AOPA's Privacy and Security Officer will make a determination about whether to amend the record(s) and notifies the requester in writing of that decision.
    • If the amendment request is granted, AOPA's privacy and security officer takes the necessary steps to amend or correct the information and notifies the requester by email or letter that the change has been made.
    • If the amendment request is denied, AOPA's Privacy and Security Officer informs the requester of such determination in writing, including the reasons for the denial and the procedures established by AOPA to appeal the decision.
    • If a request is denied, the requester has the right to file an appeal within ten (10) business days after AOPA notifies the requester that it has denied the request. No later than 30 business days from the date on which the individual requests such review, AOPA's legal department will complete such review and make a final determination unless, for good cause shown, the reviewing official extends such 30-day period; and if, after the official's review, the reviewing official also refuses to amend the record in accordance with the request, permit the user to file with AOPA a concise statement setting forth the reasons for their disagreement with the refusal of AOPA. In any disclosure to the FAA of that user's record, AOPA will provide the FAA a copy of any such statement provided by the user.

IV. SHARING PRACTICES

  1. WHO WILL AOPA SHARE THE COLLECTED INFORMATION WITH?
    AOPA will only share the information collected with the FAA as required by Section 2307 of FESSA, 14 CFR § 68.3, and AOPA's agreement with the FAA.
  2. HOW WILL AOPA SHARE THE INFORMATION WITH THE FAA?
    Upon your successful completion of AOPA's online medical education course, AOPA will perform a one-time electronic transfer to the FAA of the information collected on the form as required by Section 2307(c) of FESSA. AOPA will transmit the information collected to the FAA on the current or next federal workday, Monday through Friday.
  3. HOW WILL AOPA SECURE THE COLLECTED INFORMATION DURING TRANSMISSION TO THE FAA?
    AOPA will electronically transmit the information to the FAA through a secure FTP connection. The data will be encrypted during transit.
  4. ONCE THE INFORMATION IS TRANSMITTED TO THE FAA, IS A SYSTEMS OF RECORD APPLICABLE UNDER THE PRIVACY ACT, 5 U.S.C. 552a?
    AOPA will transmit to the FAA the information collected as required by Section 2307(c) of FESSA, and the FAA will include the information in a Privacy Act System of Records known as DOT/FAA 847, titled "Aviation Records on Individuals" and the information will be subject to the routine uses published in the System of Records Notice for DOT/FAA 847 (see www.dot.gov/privacy/privacyactnotices).

V. ADMINSTRATIVE AND TECHNOLOGICAL CONTROLS

  1. WHAT IS THE RETENTION SCHEDULE ESTABLISHED FOR THE INFORMATION COLLECTED?
    Unless otherwise expressly authorized by the user through a voluntary "opt-in" feature, AOPA will only retain the information collected upon successful completion of the medical education course until the information is transmitted to the FAA, as required by Section 2307 of FESSA and 14 CFR § 68.3. AOPA will transmit the information collected to the FAA on the current or next federal workday, Monday through Friday.

    If the individual has provided a specific "opt-in" consent for AOPA to use the name, email address, and/or address for surveys and providing feedback; AOPA will retain this information for up to 30 calendar days or until the survey has been sent, whichever comes first.

    If the individual has provided a specific "opt-in" consent for AOPA to use the name, email address, and/or address for a reminder to complete a medical education course every 24 calendar months, AOPA will retain this information for 24 calendar months.

    If the individual has provided a specific "opt-in" consent for AOPA to use the name, email address, and/or address provided by such individual to send any type of communication from AOPA, AOPA will retain this information for perpetuity or until a subsequent "opt-out" is received pursuant to the AOPA Privacy Policy found at https://www.aopa.org/privacy-policy

  2. WHAT ARE THE PROCEDURES FOR IDENTIFICATION AND DISPOSITION OF THE DATA AT THE END OF THE RETENTION PERIOD?
    Automated data scrubbing routines are executed weekly to identify and dispose of data no longer eligible for retention. Automated notifications are sent to system administrators confirming the routines have been executed and completed successfully.
  3. WHAT TYPE OF PRIVACY TRAINING IS PROVIDED TO AOPA EMPLOYEES, EITHER GENERALLY OR SPECIFICALLY RELEVANT TO THE PROGRAM OR SYSTEM?
    IT personnel are required to attend periodic refresher training on related security and privacy policies as part of continuous improvement initiatives.
  4. WILL AOPA CONTRACTORS HAVE ACCESS TO THE COLLECTED INFORMATION?
    No, unless the individual opts-in to receive any type of communication from AOPA. The information provided by these individuals will be subject to the AOPA Privacy Policy found at https://www.aopa.org/privacy-policy
  5. WHAT GROUPS AT AOPA WILL HAVE ACCESS TO THE INFORMATION COLLECTED?
    Access to information collected is limited to the Database Administrator and the Director of Infrastructure. No other groups or employees will have direct access to the information collected.
  6. HOW DOES AOPA DETERMINE WHAT GROUPS AT AOPA WILL HAVE ACCESS TO THE INFORMATION COLLECTED?
    Permission to access information collected must be approved by AOPA's Privacy and Security Officer prior to any information being released. No employee, besides the Database Administrator and the Director of Infrastructure will have direct access to the information collected. Any other groups at AOPA that are deemed appropriate to receive information, once approved, will be delivered an extraction of data specific to the approval, and with adherence to retention and privacy policies as stated above.
  7. HOW ARE THE ASSIGNMENTS OF ROLES AND RULES VERIFIED ACCORDING TO ESTABLISHED SECURITY AND AUDITING PROCEDURES?
    All assignments of roles must be approved by AOPA's Privacy and Security Officer before being established. Approved assignment roles are recorded on the employee's data access profile and will immediately be removed on termination of employment or if role is no longer required as part of the employee's current job duties.
  8. WHAT AUDITING MEASURES/CONTROLS AND TECHNICAL SAFEGUARDS ARE IN PLACE TO PREVENT MISUSE (E.G., UNAUTHORIZED BROWSING) OF DATA?
    Data access roles and policies are reviewed on an annual basis as part of a security self-assessment performed by AOPA's Privacy and Security Officer.
  9. IS ANY USER INFORMATION COLLECTED AUTOMATICALLY (I.E., USER'S IP ADDRESS, LOCATION, AND TIME OF VISIT) AND IDENTIFY THE USE FOR WHICH IT IS COLLECTED (I.E., SITE MANAGEMENT OR SECURITY PURPOSES).
    No information is collected automatically.

VI. Privacy Analysis

Given the nature of the information that is required to be collected, ensuring confidentiality of the information is a priority. AOPA has identified and mitigated the following privacy risks:

  • Data provided by individuals might not be accurate; and
  • Data provided by claimants might be misused or improperly accessed.

The privacy risks identified are mitigated by administrative, technical, and physical controls implemented by AOPA. AOPA has limited information collection to the minimum necessary to comply with Section 2307(c) of FESSA. The likelihood of erroneous information is limited because the individual user directly provides all of the information collected. No AOPA employee, besides the AOPA Database Administrator and the Director of Infrastructure will have direct access to the information collected. Permission to access information collected must be approved by AOPA's Privacy and Security Officer. All AOPA information technology personnel are required to attend periodic refresher training on related security and privacy policies as part of continuous improvement initiatives. The periodic monitoring, of logs and accounts, helps to prevent and/or discover unauthorized access attempts. Additionally, the information is stored in a physical location that is protected by escort requirements for access.

This Statement of Privacy, provided pursuant to the Privacy Act of 1974, 5 U.S.C. § 552a, applies to the FAA BasicMed online medical course and governs data collection and usage. By transmitting information using this process, you consent to the data practices described in this statement.

The information entered by a user into the BasicMed course is solicited under the authority of the FAA Extension, Safety, and Security Act of 2016, Pub. L. No. 114-190 (2016); Title 49, United States Code (U.S.C.) (Transportation) sections 106, 40113(a) and 44701-44703, formerly codified in the Federal Aviation Act of 1958, as amended; and Title 14, Code of Federal Regulations (CFR), part 68, Requirements for Operating Certain Small Aircraft Without a Medical Certificate.

Collection of Your Personal Information

The information system supporting BasicMed collects personally identifiable information, such as your e-mail address, name, address, and telephone number.

Use of Your Personal Information

The FAA collects your personal information to identify you as a BasicMed participant in your airman record.

Except for your date of birth (DOB) and email address, submission of this information is mandatory to exercise pilot privileges under BasicMed. Incomplete submission will result in delay of eligibility to exercise pilot privileges under BasicMed. Other than the optional information requested, the purpose of the information is to establish that you meet Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) requirements to operate under BasicMed. Submission of your DOB or email is not required by law and is voluntary. Refusal to furnish DOB or email will not result in the denial of any right, benefit, or privilege provided by law. Your DOB and email is solicited to assist in performing the agency's functions under 49 U.S.C. (Transportation). If supplied, it may be used by the FAA to associate all information in agency files relating to you.

The information becomes part of the FAA Privacy Act system of records, DOT/FAA-847, Aviation Records on Individuals, as published in the Federal Register at 75 FR 68849 (Nov. 9, 2010), also available at https://www.gpo.gov/fdsys/pkg/FR-2010-11-09/pdf/2010-28237.pdf. These records and information in these records may be disclosed in accordance with the routine uses that appear in DOT/FAA-847, which include the following: (a) to provide basic airman certification and qualification information to the public upon request; (b) to disclose information to the National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) in connection with its investigation responsibilities; (c) to provide information about airmen to Federal, state, and local law enforcement agencies when engaged in the investigation and apprehension of drug law violators; (d) to provide information about enforcement actions arising out of violations of the Federal Aviation Regulations to government agencies, the aviation industry, and the public upon request; (e) to disclose information to another Federal agency, or to a court or an administrative tribunal, when the Government or one of its agencies is a party to a judicial proceeding before the court or involved in administrative proceedings before the tribunal; (f) to disclose information to other Federal agencies for verification of the accuracy or completeness of the information; and (g) to comply with the Prefatory Statement of General Routine Uses for the Department of Transportation.

By consenting to the required NDR check under BasicMed, you permit the FAA to request information, if any, pertaining to your driving record from the National Driver Register (NDR). The FAA will verify the NDR information it receives with the state of record. You have the right to request an NDR file check to determine if it contains any information and, if so, the accuracy of such information. Notarized requests may be sent to: DOT/NHTSA/NTS-32, 400 7th Street, S.W., Washington, DC 20590-0001, and must contain your complete name and date of birth. Other information about height, weight, and eye color will ensure correct positive identification.

Security of Your Personal Information

Properly securing the information we collect online is a primary commitment. To help us do this, the FAA takes the following steps, once data is submitted to it, to: (a) employ internal access controls to ensure the only people who see your information are those with a need to do so to perform their official duties; (b) train relevant personnel on our privacy and security measures to know requirements for compliance; (c) secure the areas where we hold hard copies of information we collect online; (d) perform regular backups of the information we collect online to insure against loss; (e) use technical controls to secure the information we collect online, including but not limited to Secure Socket Layer (SSL), encryption, firewalls, and password protections; (f) periodically test our security procedures to ensure personnel and technical compliance; (g) employ external access safeguards to identify and prevent unauthorized attempts of outsiders to hack into, or cause harm to, the information in our systems. Tampering with FAA's website is against the law. Depending on the offense, it is punishable under the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act of 1986 and the National Information Infrastructure Protection Act. General Aviation stakeholder groups providing a BasicMed online course have entered into agreements with the FAA to take identical steps to protect your information while your data is in their possession.

Changes to this Statement

The FAA will occasionally update this Statement of Privacy to reflect FAA and customer feedback. The FAA encourages you to periodically review this statement to be informed of how the FAA is protecting your information.
